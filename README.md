# System-Analysis-and-Design-

1. Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
DriverPass is a webbased solution to scheduling driving lessons with an instructor, taking practice tests online, taking test prep lessons, and purchasing lesson packages. This was a design focused on modernizing an existing business.

2. What did you do particularly well?
I designed a new system for this company, achieving all of their needs. This includes purchasing online lesson packages, taking practice tests, and scheduling in-person lessons.

3. If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
It was not a requirement to add methods in the Class Diagram, but I think if I went back and added that it would make the diagram more useful.

4. How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I made sure to reread the initial meeting document to ensure that I knew everything the client wanted. This is critical, as their needs and desires are the only thing that ultimately matter. I can design a perfect system that doesn't meet their criteria and they will be unable to use it.

5. How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
Alway focus on the business requirements. It is crucial to make sure you are answer their questions and needs and not creating new problems.
